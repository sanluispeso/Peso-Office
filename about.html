About PESO

The Public Employment Service Office (PESO) of San Luis, Aurora serves as a bridge between job seekers and employers. It provides employment facilitation, career guidance, and livelihood programs to promote local employment and economic growth.

Vision & Mission

History of the Public Employment Service Office (PESO) in the Municipality of San Luis, Aurora

The establishment and strengthening of the Public Employment Service Office (PESO) in the Municipality of San Luis, Aurora is rooted in the national government’s commitment to institutionalizing employment facilitation services across local government units (LGUs), as mandated by Republic Act No. 10691.

Under Section 1 of RA 10691, all provinces, cities, and municipalities are required to establish, operate, and maintain a PESO under the Office of the Governor, City Mayor, or Municipal Mayor. The law envisions PESO as a frontline service office responsible for linking job seekers with employment opportunities, supporting workforce development, and promoting livelihood programs at the local level.

Consistent with this mandate, the Municipality of San Luis, Aurora began organizing its local employment facilitation services with the designation of a PESO Manager. On February 6, 2024, Hon. Ariel A. De Jesus, Municipal Mayor of San Luis, Aurora, formally designated Ms. Joanna Pauline A. Raymundo as PESO Manager. Her appointment marked the operational beginning of the municipality’s PESO functions.

As PESO Manager, Ms. Raymundo was tasked with facilitating labor market information exchange between job seekers and employers, providing employment services for both local and overseas work, and assisting employers in recruitment. The office also undertook referral services for livelihood and self-employment programs, conducted employability enhancement trainings and seminars, offered career guidance and counseling, provided pre-employment orientation for overseas workers, developed testing and evaluation tools, and extended reintegration assistance to returning migrant workers.

To further institutionalize employment services within the municipal government, the Sangguniang Bayan of San Luis enacted Municipal Ordinance No. 005-2024 during its regular session on April 30, 2024. Anchored on the provisions of Republic Act No. 7160, or the Local Government Code of 1991, the ordinance formally created the position of Senior Labor and Employment Officer (Salary Grade 19).

The creation of this position marked a significant milestone in the development of the Public Employment Service Office in San Luis, Aurora. What began as a designated function evolved into a structured and institutionalized local government service, reinforcing the municipality’s role in promoting decent employment, economic mobility, and inclusive community development.
